Pulav


Discription


Veg Pulao is a popular, comforting dish from Indian cuisine. It is a one-pot meal that consists of rice, vegetables, spices, and herbs. The key to a good pulao is fluffy, separated grains of rice with the distinct aroma of whole spices like bay leaf, cardamom, and cloves, along with the fresh taste of mint and coriander.


Ingredients


    1½ cups Rice.
    2½ cups water (use 3 cups for a softer texture, or 2 cups for a pressure cooker)
    2 tablespoons Ghee or Oil
    1 medium Onion, thinly sliced
    1 cup Mixed Vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, potatoes, cauliflower), chopped into small, even pieces
    1-2 Green Chilies, slit
    1½ teaspoon Ginger-Garlic Paste
    A handful of fresh Coriander leaves, chopped
    A few Mint leaves (optional)
    Salt to taste 1 Bay Leaf, 1-inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini), 4 Cloves (Laung), 4 Green Cardamoms (Elaichi), ½ teaspoon Cumin Seeds (Jeera)

Steps:

    1. Rinse and Soak Rice: Rinse 1½ cups basmati rice 3-4 times until the water runs clear. Soak in water for 20–30 minutes. Drain completely and set aside.
    2. Heat Ghee & Temper Spices: Heat 2 tbsp ghee or oil in a pressure cooker or a heavy-bottomed pot. Add whole spices (cumin, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, star anise) and sauté for 30 seconds until aromatic.
    3. Onions & Aromatics: Add sliced onions and slit green chilies. Sauté on medium heat until the onions turn light golden brown. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
    4. Add Veggies & Herbs: Add the chopped vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, potatoes) and mint/coriander leaves. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until they start to soften slightly.
    5. Toast the Rice: Add the drained rice and sauté gently for 1-2 minutes, stirring carefully to avoid breaking the grains. This coats the rice in ghee, ensuring it stays separate.
    6. Add Water & Cook: Pour in 2½ cups of water (or stock) and add salt. The water should taste slightly salty.
    7. Rest & Serve: Once cooked, let the pulao rest for 10 minutes, then gently fluff with a fork. Garnish with coriander leaves.
